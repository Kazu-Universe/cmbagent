## Task

Determine whether any paper published in 2020 or later establishes, via
heat-kernel/one-loop matter-field computations, that the logarithmic-in-area
correction to black hole entropy is universal across matter content
(scalar, fermion, vector, higher-spin) — as opposed to being spin/matter-
content-dependent, per the pre-2020 baseline (Sen et al., arXiv:1005.3044).
Iliesiu–Turiaci (arXiv:2003.02860) is explicitly excluded as resolving this
question, since it concerns the Schwarzian near-extremal zero-mode sector —
a structurally different question. The Wald-entropy/anomaly-coefficient
sense of "universal" (coefficient fixed by a given field's own central-
charge data) is explicitly distinguished from cross-matter-content
universality throughout.

## Search Mode: LIVE

Queries executed against the real INSPIRE-HEP API (`https://inspirehep.net/api/literature`)
across two rounds, 29 distinct query phrasings plus 8 additional sort-order/
identifier-verification queries in round 2 (37 total), covering direct
topic terms, heat-kernel/Seeley-DeWitt terminology, spin/matter-content
language, Wald-entropy/anomaly terminology, and targeted author/topic
combinations. The single most on-topic query ("quantum entropy function
logarithmic correction black hole") returned 74 total hits and was screened
under three independent sort orders (`mostrecent`, `mostcited`, `bestmatch`,
top 25 each) to maximize coverage given the tool's lack of a pagination/
offset parameter.

**Round 2 addition:** two previously-missed, directly on-topic 2020 papers
were surfaced by the `bestmatch` sort and individually verified via
targeted `arxiv:` identifier queries:

- **arXiv:2012.12227** — Karan & Panda, "Logarithmic corrections to black
  hole entropy in matter coupled N≥1 Einstein-Maxwell supergravity" (JHEP
  05 (2021) 104). Computes Seeley-DeWitt heat-kernel coefficients for
  massless field fluctuations across different N=2 Einstein-Maxwell-
  supergravity multiplets.
- **arXiv:2007.11497** — Banerjee, Karan & Panda, "Logarithmic correction
  to the entropy of extremal black holes in N=1 Einstein-Maxwell
  supergravity" (JHEP 01 (2021) 090). Same heat-kernel/QEF method applied
  to N=1 non-minimally-coupled Einstein-Maxwell supergravity.

Both are squarely in the Sen-type quantum-entropy-function/heat-kernel
tradition — they compute Seeley-DeWitt coefficients specific to a given
theory's matter multiplet content and derive that theory's own log-
correction coefficient. Neither claims or proves the coefficient is the
same across different matter representations. This extends, rather than
overturns, the matter-content-*dependent* structure of 1005.3044.

## Established vs. Contested

- **Established (pre-2020, settled within its formalism):** Sen and
  collaborators' quantum-entropy-function heat-kernel computations
  (1005.3044 and follow-ups) show the log-in-area coefficient is computed
  from the zero-mode/non-zero-mode heat-kernel content of the *specific*
  matter multiplet coupled to the background, and is generally different
  across different matter content. "Universal" in this literature's own
  usage means macroscopic effective-action data reproducing microscopic
  string-theoretic degeneracy counting — **not** matter-content-independence.
- **Established but a distinct notion (Wald/anomaly sense):** 2104.14902 —
  coefficient tied to a given field's own conformal anomaly/central-charge
  data, not a claim of cross-matter equality.
- **Established but a third distinct notion (string-compactification
  universal sector):** 2210.16230 — "universal" refers to the dilaton/
  graviton/moduli sector common to any compactification, unrelated to
  cross-matter-content universality of the log coefficient.
- **Reinforced by round 2 (2020+, still matter-content-dependent):**
  2012.12227 and 2007.11497 extend the same Seeley-DeWitt/heat-kernel/QEF
  method to N≥1 and N=1 Einstein-Maxwell supergravity multiplets — each
  computes a coefficient specific to that theory's particular field
  content, further exemplifying rather than resolving the matter-
  dependence Sen's original result established.
- **Contested/unresolved (the actual question asked):** no paper found
  proves cross-matter-content universality (scalar = fermion = vector =
  higher-spin coefficient equality) via heat-kernel/one-loop computation in
  2020 or later.
- **Explicitly excluded:** Iliesiu-Turiaci (2003.02860) — Schwarzian/near-
  extremal zero-mode sector, correctly not counted toward this question.

## Results

Across 37 live query phrasings (29 in round 1, 8 additional sort-order/
identifier queries in round 2) and three independent sort orders applied to
the single most on-topic 74-hit query, **~58 of 74 unique records (~78%
coverage)** were directly screened; **~16 records (~22%) were never viewed
under any of the three sort orders**, since the tool exposes no pagination/
offset parameter for a fourth distinct view. Given the composition of what
*was* screened — overwhelmingly unrelated modified-gravity/LQG/GUP papers
with only a handful of genuinely on-topic Sen-line entries — the marginal
risk that a missed universality-proof paper sits in the unscreened ~22% is
assessed as low, though this is a qualitative judgment, not a completeness
guarantee.

**No paper published in 2020 or later was found in INSPIRE's index that
proves, via heat-kernel/one-loop matter computations, that the log-in-area
correction to black hole entropy is universal across matter content.** All
identified 2020+ papers on log corrections either (i) extend Sen's matter-
content-dependent quantum-entropy-function framework to new specific
theories/multiplets, continuing to find content-dependent coefficients, or
(ii) use "universal" in one of the two distinct senses identified above
(Wald/anomaly-coefficient; string-compactification universal sector). The
pre-2020 baseline (Sen et al., 1005.3044) remains the standing statement
that the log coefficient is matter/spin-content dependent, and nothing in
the 2020+ literature indexed by INSPIRE, as screened here, contradicts or
supersedes this via a heat-kernel proof of cross-matter universality.

## Disclosed Gaps and Tool Limitations

- **Coverage:** ~22% of the single most on-topic 74-hit query's results
  were never directly viewed under any of the three sort orders used
  (see corrected figure above) — not a "small residual," a real ~1-in-5
  gap, though the composition argument above bounds the plausible risk.
- **Date-filter unreliability, confirmed empirically:** the `date <range>`
  clause in this tool's query syntax was demonstrated, at least three
  independent times in this session, to be a no-op — queries with an
  explicit `date 2020-2025` filter returned papers dated as early as 2006,
  well outside the stated window. **The date filter should not be relied
  upon anywhere in this domain**; any date-scoping must be done by manually
  inspecting each hit's actual publication date, not by trusting the query
  clause.
- **Citation-graph tooling non-functional:** `refersto:`/`citedby:` query
  clauses did not work in this session, preventing a systematic "everything
  citing 1005.3044 since 2020" sweep as an independent completeness check.
- **No pagination/offset parameter** is exposed by this tool, which is why
  three separate sort orders (rather than direct pagination) were used to
  maximize coverage of the 74-hit query.
- The live search covers INSPIRE's indexed literature only; it cannot rule
  out an obscure or very recent (unindexed) preprint not yet on INSPIRE.
- No paper was found explicitly stating the *negative* result either — the
  2020+ literature continues applying the content-dependent framework
  without engaging "is it universal across matter content" as a stated
  question to be resolved either way.
